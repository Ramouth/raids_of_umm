Combat Philosophy: The "Moving Castle" Tactical Engine

This design evolves the classic HoMM "Army vs. Army" formula into a high-stakes, Grid-Based Tactics game. It blends the scale of stack-based warfare with the mechanical depth of Early MMORPGs and the spatial precision of D&D.

I. The Core Pillars

    Army-Scale Tactics: Combat remains a clash of massive stacks, but victory is determined by geometry, not just math.

    Spatial Significance: Using a Hex Grid, movement and orientation (Front vs. Flank) dictate damage mitigation and counter-attack opportunities. Six clearly defined neighbors make flanking unambiguous and positional decisions legible — the same reason XCOM's hex grid never feels limiting.

    The High-Stakes "Glass Cannon": Special Characters act as your "Raid Group." They possess game-changing power but are fragile and carry Wondrous Items that are lost upon death.

II. Special Characters & Wondrous Items

Unlike standard units, Special Characters function as Force Multipliers. Their power is tied to their equipment, which introduces Geometric Attack Patterns defined as sets of relative hex offsets.

    The Shape of Power: Attacks are no longer just 1×1. Items allow for 1×3 Lines along a hex axis, 60° Wedges (3-hex cones), or Ring-shaped heals (the 6 neighbors of a target hex).

    MMO Archetypes:

        Tanks: Use "Geometric Taunts" (hex cones) to lock enemy aggro and "Shield Walls" to provide cover for the stacks behind them.

        Healers: Must position themselves precisely to hit multiple friendly stacks with "Chain" (line) or "Cross" (center + neighbors) patterns.

        Glass Cannons: Can "Channel" the power of a nearby 500-unit stack into a single, devastating geometric strike — the stack cannot act that turn and contributes a damage multiplier based on its size.

    The Risk: If a Special Character dies, their item drops or shatters. This creates an "Extraction" tension—do you push for the win or retreat to save your legendary loot?

III. Advanced Tactical Interactions

The system introduces "Intelligible Tactics" to make the battlefield feel alive and reactive.

    Flanking: If two units occupy opposite hex sides of an enemy (distance-2 neighbors through the target), that enemy is "Pinned." They lose their Counter-Attack and take 150% damage. With only 6 hex directions, "opposite" is perfectly unambiguous — making Turning a unit a primary tactical goal.

    Zone of Control (ZoC): Heavy units "stick" to enemies. To reach a Glass Cannon, an enemy must either kill the Tank or take a massive "Attack of Opportunity" to move past.

    The "Long Cast" (Telegraphed Moves): The most powerful MMO-style moves (like a Starfall AoE) take one full turn to "charge." The affected hex cells are highlighted and the enemy must either move out of the way or kill the caster before the start of the next round.

IV. The "Moving Castle" Gameplay Loop

Strategy revolves around a formation-based approach. The player manages a "Moving Castle":

    The Shell: Sturdy infantry stacks and Tanks form the exterior to block flanking.

    The Core: Healers and Glass Cannons sit in the center, protected from direct engagement.

    The Hero: Acts as the "Controller," using global magic to teleport units, create terrain walls, or "Cleanse" debuffs from Special Characters.

    Design Goal: To create a "Hardcore" tactical experience where every hex of movement matters, and the loss of a single character feels like losing a piece of your soul—or at least your favorite sword.
