# Raids of Umm'Natur — Factions

> **Six factions. One city. One Crown.**
> Every faction has a different reason to be at Pha'raxh.
> None of them fully understand what the others are.

---

## The World — The Fall of Pha'raxh

The great desert empire of Pha'raxh did not fall to an outside invader.
It destroyed itself.

For centuries the empire was the most powerful force on the continent —
commanding Djinns, wielding necromantic power no other civilisation could
explain, ruled by pharaohs who seemed to transcend death itself. What nobody
on the surface knew was that the empire was a front. The pharaohs were a
public face. The real power lived beneath the city — an ancient subterranean
civilisation called **The Veined**, who had engineered the empire as a mask
for their own existence.

It worked until the pharaohs stopped believing they were masks.

After generations of wielding borrowed power, the human nobility came to
believe the power was theirs. They revolted against the foundations of their
own city. The war that followed was total — human armies driving into the
tunnels, The Veined consuming everything they could reach on the surface.
It destroyed both sides. Pha'raxh collapsed. The empire ended.
The Veined retreated underground. The humans who were not at the centre
of the war — the desert tribes, the peripheral peoples — survived.

That was generations ago.

Now the city sits abandoned in the deep desert. And everyone has heard
about the Crown.

---

## The Crown of Pha'raxh

The most powerful artifact ever created — a crown that commands all Djinns.
Forged by The Veined, worn by puppet pharaohs, the source of the empire's
dominance. It was sealed inside Pha'raxh when the city fell.

The **Eye of Djangjix** — shards of crystallised Djinn essence scattered
across the desert's dungeons — are the keys to entering the Crown's vault.
No faction can reach the Crown without assembling the Eye first.

**Win conditions:**

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Military** | Eliminate all rival factions' towns and heroes |
| **Crown of Pha'raxh** | Assemble the Eye shards, enter the vault, claim the Crown |

The Crown win requires pushing deep into contested dungeons while enemies
pressure you from every direction. The Military win is slower but does not
require entering Pha'raxh at all.

## Faction 1 — The Veined

> *"We were here before you built your city. We will be here after you are gone."*

### Identity

Not undead. Not necromancers. Something older and stranger.

The Veined are a subterranean civilisation that has lived beneath the desert
for millennia. Their bodies are dark-skinned and vampiric in appearance —
striking, dangerous, ancient — with mineral veins visibly threaded beneath
the skin. They absorb organic material that filters down through the desert
above: dead animals, fallen soldiers, buried pharaohs. This is not magic.
It is their biology.

They have no concept of death as loss. Everything that dies eventually
becomes part of them.

The empire of Pha'raxh was their creation — a human mask for an underground
civilisation that had no desire to be seen. The pharaohs were collaborators
who, over generations, forgot they were collaborators. The revolt destroyed
the mask. The Veined retreated, absorbed the casualties of the war, and waited.

Now they are surfacing for the first time. Most of the world has never seen them.

### Why they are at Pha'raxh

The Crown is theirs. They provided the death-essence that gave it power.
It is made of their material. They want it returned — not to rule with it,
but because it is part of them in a way no other faction can claim.

### Combat Feel

**Attrition and consumption.** The longer a fight lasts, the more dangerous
The Veined become. Active consumption — specific units can consume adjacent
corpses (enemy or allied) to heal and grow. Positioning to feed is the
tactical core.

They are not fast. They do not need to be. They win wars of exhaustion.
Hit them hard, hit them fast, end it early — or lose.

## Faction 2 — The Ivory Compact

> *"We bring order to what was abandoned. The Crown belongs to those who can use it."*

### Identity

A northern military alliance — professional soldiers, disciplined cavalry,
siege engineers. No magic, no necromancy. Steel, logistics, and the belief
that power belongs to whoever is organised enough to hold it.

They heard about the Crown through trade intelligence. They have no emotional
or historical connection to Pha'raxh. They are here because commanding Djinns
means controlling the continent, and they intend to be first.

### Why they are at Pha'raxh

Political dominance. Whoever holds the Crown rewrites the balance of power
across every kingdom. The Ivory Compact's leadership has calculated that
this is worth any cost.

### Combat Feel

**Combined arms and map control.** The most reliable faction. Cavalry,
ranged units, heavy infantry — each supporting the others. No single
spectacular ability. Wins by out-positioning, denying resources, and
applying consistent pressure the opponent cannot ignore.

The faction for players who want to win through strategy rather than
a specific power spike.

## Faction 3 — The Djinn Courts

> *"We were here before the sand. The Crown is a scar on the world. We will close it."*

### Identity

Elemental spirits of wind, sand, and storm. They predate the empire,
predate the city, predate most of what the surface factions call history.
The desert is not their home — they are the desert's animating force.

The Crown is an existential threat. It does not merely command Djinns —
it *enslaves* them, strips them of will and binds them to mortal purpose.
The Djinn Courts know what the Crown is made of now. The Veined made it
from Djinn-essence extracted by force. The Court has not forgotten.

They want the Crown destroyed. Not claimed. Not studied. Gone.

### Why they are at Pha'raxh

Survival. If the Crown reaches a ruler who knows how to use it, the Djinn
Courts cease to exist as a free civilisation.

### Combat Feel

**Glass cannon timing.** Smallest army, highest individual power. Everything
flies. Magic bypass on all attacks — armour is irrelevant against them.
Loses any war of attrition. Wins by ending the fight before the opponent
can stabilise.

Must leverage mobility to dictate engagement terms. Cannot hold territory.
Cannot absorb losses. Wins wars of timing, loses wars of endurance.

### Economy Emphasis

Crystal + Obsidian

## Faction 4 — The Sylvan Host

> *"We came to end it. We did not come to become it."*

### Identity

Elves from a land far from the desert. They followed traces of buried
forest-magic — ancient growth that was suffocated when the desert spread
over what was once fertile land. They believe the Crown's power is what
accelerated that desertification. Destroy the Crown and the land begins
to heal.

They are the most morally certain faction at the start of the game.
They are the most at risk of becoming something else by the end.

### Corruption Mechanic

The deeper the Sylvan Host pushes into the ruins, the more the old
Veined-necromantic residue affects them. This is not magic cast upon them —
it is simply what happens when living things spend too long in places
saturated with absorbed death.

Individual SCs can be corrupted. A corrupted SC's skill tree shifts —
abilities change, appearance changes, and their relationship with their
faction changes. A fully corrupted Sylvan SC is no longer playing the
same game they started.

The corruption arc is the Sylvan Host's primary narrative tension.

### Why they are at Pha'raxh

Destroy the Crown. Restore the land. Go home.

### Combat Feel

**Ranged dominance and guerrilla.** Punishes movement in the open.
Devastating at range, fragile in direct contact. Excels at ambush and
terrain denial. Forces opponents to fight on the Sylvan Host's terms
or bleed trying to close distance.

### Economy Emphasis

## Faction 5 — The Iron Conclave

> *"We built it. We know what is inside. That is precisely why no one else can have it."*

### Identity

An ancient order of engineers and architects — not a nation, not an army,
but a guild so old and so capable that empires hire them to build their
greatest works. They built Pha'raxh. Every vault, every passage, every
trap, every foundation stone. They have the maps.

They also know what is underneath. They saw The Veined when they dug
the foundations. They took the payment and kept building and said nothing.

For generations that secret was safe because the city was destroyed and
no one was going back. Now everyone is going back. The Conclave's elders
have mobilised for the first time in living memory — not to reclaim power,
but to get inside that city and seal it before anyone else can start asking
the right questions.

### Why they are at Pha'raxh

Damage control. The Conclave's complicity in the greatest deception in
history is one conversation away from being exposed. They want the Crown
locked in their mountain vaults, the city sealed, and the subject closed
permanently.

### Combat Feel

**Siege and denial.** The slowest faction on the map. Individually the
toughest units in the game. Wins by making territory impassable —
fortifications, defensive structures, chokepoint control.

Also the only faction with architectural knowledge of Pha'raxh itself —
mechanically this translates to fog-of-war advantages inside the city
and movement bonuses through ruins terrain.

## Faction 6 — The Shariw

> *"You came for a city. We are the city's memory."*

### Identity

The Shariw are the desert's survivors — the people who were in the empire's
periphery when Pha'raxh destroyed itself. Not powerful enough to be useful
to The Veined. Not wealthy enough to be targets. They lived in the open
desert, in tribes, out of the eye of the storm.

They speak Pha'raxhi — the old language, kept alive in oral tradition
while the civilisation that invented it burned. Their tales carry fragments
of what the empire really was: stories of pharaohs who did not die the
way humans die, of things that moved beneath the sand at night, of a city
that was older than its own foundation stones. Not history. Tales. Close
enough to the truth to be dangerous to the right listeners.

An individual Shariw is a **Shariw'a**. The people collectively are
**the Shariw**.

### Why they are at Pha'raxh

It is their home. Not in the sense of a political claim — in the sense
that their grandparents' grandparents walked these dunes, knew these ruins,
and called this desert theirs before any outside faction drew a map of it.

They do not want the Crown. They want everyone else gone. Their win
condition is reclaiming Pha'raxh — driving out the factions, sealing the
city with proper ritual, and letting the desert take it back. The Crown
stays underground. The dead stay dead. The land rests.

### Combat Feel

**Terrain mastery and information.** Not the strongest army, not the most
magical. But the Shariw move through desert terrain that slows every other
faction, start with more of the map revealed, and can place units in
ambush positions that other factions cannot access.

Their strength is knowing where the enemy is before the enemy knows where
they are. Their weakness is that when caught in open direct combat, they
cannot match the heavier factions unit-for-unit.

## The Djinn Manifest

Any faction can summon a Djinn as a legendary Special Character — one per game.
**Manifest Fragments** are scattered in the highest-tier dungeons. All fragments
must be carried to a designated **Ritual Town** in the contested center of the map.
The summoning is a town action, not instant — the hero is exposed and stationary
at their most powerful moment.

The summoned Djinn is bound to the hero who performed the ritual.
It cannot be transferred. It is the strongest individual entity in the game.

## Open Questions

1. **Corruption mechanic scope** — does Sylvan Host corruption affect only SCs,
   or can regular units also shift? At what point does a corrupted Sylvan player
   gain access to Veined-adjacent abilities?

2. **Shariw win condition mechanics** — "drive out all factions and seal the city"
   needs a concrete game action. Is it a special building they construct in Pha'raxh?
   A ritual requiring specific resources?

3. **Iron Conclave map knowledge** — how is the fog-of-war advantage implemented?
   Partial reveal at game start? Faster movement in ruins? Both?

4. **The Shariw elder SC and the truth** — at what point in the campaign does a
   Shariw'a piece together that the tales of "ones who transcend being human"
   describe The Veined? Campaign story beat, not a multiplayer mechanic.

5. **Veined first contact** — the Ivory Compact soldiers encountering The Veined
   for the first time is a major narrative moment. Needs a dedicated mission or cutscene.

6. **SC slots** — Sylvan Host, Iron Conclave all have TBD SCs. Djinn Courts has 2 TBD.
   Shariw has 3 TBD. To be filled in during campaign/SC design pass.

7. **Unit resource costs** — all 42 units need gold + specialty resource costs assigned,
   aligned to their faction's economy emphasis. Next major data task before implementation.

8. **Unit rename pass** — all names are provisional. To be done once the full roster
   is validated in playtesting.
